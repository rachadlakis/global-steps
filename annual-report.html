2025 Annual Report

A year of partnership, persistence, and progress.

Our 12th annual report covers programs, impact, financials, and what we learned.

Download PDF (4.2 MB) Read online

Published February 2026

Letter from our Executive Director

2025 was a year of consolidation. After three years of rapid growth we slowed down to deepen relationships with our existing partners and rebuild our monitoring practices. The result: tighter programs, better evidence, and — for the first time in our history — outcome data that we can stand behind across every program.

We also said no to several large opportunities that didn't align with our community-led principles. That kind of restraint isn't free — it cost us roughly $1.2M in declined funding. We believe it was worth it.

Programs

Across our four portfolios, we worked with 52 community-led partners in 25 countries.

Education

2,400 scholarships, 38 digital labs, 9 adult literacy programs. Independent evaluation in three countries showed a 28% improvement in reading proficiency among scholarship recipients.

Sustainability

1,200 hectares reforested, 14 solar microgrids commissioned, 18 climate-resilient farming cohorts trained.

Community Development

1,800 microenterprise grants disbursed. The women-led business accelerator graduated its third cohort.

Health

65,000 patients reached through mobile clinics. Two new maternal-health programs launched in Bangladesh and Senegal.

Financials

Revenue: $14.6M (foundations 62%, individual donors 28%, corporate 8%, earned 2%)

Expenses: $13.1M (programs 87%, operations 9%, fundraising 4%)

Net assets: $4.8M (includes 9-month operating reserve)

Full financials, audit, and Form 990 available at the bottom of this page.

What didn't work

Our pilot textbook program in two regions did not improve learning outcomes. We've ended it and reallocated the funding to teacher development.

Our crypto-donation channel underperformed expectations and cost more to operate than it raised. We're continuing it on a cost-recovery basis only.

Looking ahead

In 2026 we'll launch a new climate-resilience fund in partnership with the Mott Foundation, deepen our work in West Africa, and begin a long-overdue refresh of our organizational strategy.
